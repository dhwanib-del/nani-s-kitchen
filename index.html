Nani's kitchen

The things that always found their way to the counter

Not really recipes in the formal sense. Just the bowls and little mixes that kept showing up at the right time.

Things that were always around

  • Haldi + besan + milk

    The kind of paste that appeared before a bath, mixed quickly in a steel bowl and spread without much fuss.

  • Aloe vera

    Usually cut straight from the plant, cool on the skin, and brought out whenever someone said their face felt tired.

  • Coconut oil

    Always within reach, warmed between the hands, and used for everything from dry patches to weekend champi.

  • Rice water

    Saved from the pot instead of thrown away, then left by the sink because Nani believed useful things should never be wasted.

When something went wrong

  • Neem-based mixes

    Bitter, a little stubborn, and exactly what came out when skin was acting up and nothing else seemed convincing.

  • Multani mitti + rose water

    For hot days, oily foreheads, or that feeling when your skin just needed to be left alone and cooled down.

  • Chandan + yogurt

    Soft, calming, and usually suggested in the same voice that said not to touch your face so much.

Special occasions

  • Saffron + milk

    Brought out more carefully, in smaller amounts, for weddings, festivals, and the kind of evenings that felt worth dressing up for.

  • Haldi + besan + milk, again

    Because some things belonged to celebration too, especially when the whole house smelled like preparation.
